Description :

Naan, a quintessential component of Indian cuisine,is a type of leavened flatbread originating from the Indian subcontinent. Its origins can be traced back to the Persian influence on Indian culinary traditions. Typically made from wheat flour, water, yeast, and yogurt, naan dough is kneaded, rested, and then rolled into thin rounds before being baked in a tandoor, a cylindrical clay oven. The intense heat of the tandoor gives naan its characteristic bubbly texture and slightly charred flavor, making it a beloved accompaniment to various Indian dishes.
Naan comes in a variety of flavors and styles, with garlic naan and butter naan being among the most popular variations. Garlic naan is infused with minced garlic and often topped with cilantro, while butter naan is brushed with ghee or clarified butter after baking, adding richness and flavor. Naan's versatility extends beyond its use as a side dish; it can also serve as a base for wraps, sandwiches, or even pizza-like creations, showcasing its adaptability and widespread appeal in both traditional and fusion cuisines.

Ingredients :

  • Wheat flour
  • Water
  • Yeast
  • Yogurt

Steps :

  1. Mix wheat flour, water, yeast, and yogurt in a bowl to form a dough.
  2. Knead the dough until it is smooth and elastic.
  3. Cover the dough and let it rest for about 1-2 hours, allowing it to rise.
  4. Divide the dough into small portions and roll each portion into a thin round shape.
  5. Preheat a tandoor or oven to a high temperature.
  6. Place the rolled naan dough onto the hot surface and bake until it puffs up and develops a slight char on the surface.
  7. Remove the naan from the tandoor or oven and brush with butter or ghee for added flavor.
  8. Serve hot and enjoy with your favorite Indian dish.

Savor the delightful taste of Indian naan, a culinary delight that elevates every meal with its fluffy texture and buttery aroma.
